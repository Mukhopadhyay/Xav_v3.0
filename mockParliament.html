Mock Parliament

They say there is nothing like a perfect chemistry on the dance floor. Do you think you have that chemistry? Can your moves bring colours of joy to our stage? if so, then this time around as we invite you to grab your dance, win over our audience and twirl your way to victory!
Venue: FR. Depelchin auditorium (tentative)
Number of participants: 2 (maximum of 1 ca allowed)
Structure:
xtacy: teams have to perform on a pre-recorded medley of songs from any genre for the given time limit. (3+1 minutes [including setup and clearance]).
(it should be a trailer of their final performance).
Xavotsav: teams have to perform on a pre-recorded medley of songs from any genre for the given time limit (4+1 minutes [including setup and clearance]).
(extended version of the trailer in xtacy).

Rules:
  • PARTICIPANTS MAY PERFORM ON ANY DANCE STYLE OF THEIR CHOICE. DANCING ON HINDI NUMBERS IN ANY OF THE ABOVE FORMS ARE ALLOWED.
  • ALL KIND OF SONGS ARE ALLOWED. PARTRICIPANTS MAY USE MORE THAN ONE SONG IN THEIR PERFORMANCE. EVEN REMIXES ARE ALLOWED.
  • USAGE OF PROPS IS ALLOWED. THESE ARE TO BE BROUGHT BY THE TEAMS THEMSELVES. THE ORGANIZERS HOLD THE DISCRETION TO ALLOW THE PROP ON STAGE.
  • TEAMS ARE RE4UESTED TO SUBMIT THE PROP LIST PRIOR TO THE COMPETITION. THE ORGANI=ERS SHALL NOT BEAR ANY RESPONSIBILITY IN CASE OF LOSS OR DAMAGE OF PROP MATERIALS.
  • PRE-RECORDED MUSIC SHALL BE BROUGHT IN A PEN DRIVE IN .MP3 FORMAT ONLY IN A CD (AS OTHER FORMATS MAY NOT BE SUPPRTED ON THE COMPUTER USE THEM AT YOUR OWN RISK).
  • PARTICIPANTS MUST STRICTLY ADHERE TO THE TIME LIMIT.
  • ONLY ONE TEAM PER COLLEGE IS ALLOWED
  • PERFORMERS HAVE TO CLEAR THE STAGE AFTER THEIR PERFORMANCE OTHERWISE, THEY WILL BE PENALIZED HEAVILY BY THE ORGANIZERS.
  • ANY KIND OF FLUID POWDER FLAME OR HEAVY OBJECT IS NOT ALLOWED ON STAGE.
  • OB SCENITY OF ANY KIND IS NOT ALLOWED AND WILL LEAD TO IMMEDIATE DISQUALIFICATION.
  • THE DECISIONS OF THE JUDGES SHALL BE FINAL AND BINDING.
judging criteria:
  • Choreography
  • usage of costumes and props
  • choice of proper music
  • audience response
  • expression
  • synchronization
  • chemistry between the partners (coordination)
